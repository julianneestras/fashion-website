WINTER FASHION TRENDS

When it comes to your winter clothing, the most important priority should be to keep yourself warm. That doesn't mean you can't have fun with your winter wardrobe. Silk underwear, a merino wool turtleneck, and leggings—thin, moisture-wicking staples that keep you warm without making you sweat—could be your base layer. For warmth, a heavy fleece can be used as your middle layer.

SMART CASUAL

The smart casual dress code compromises casual and formal attire, making it perfect for professional social engagements like after-work socializing or a social function during a business conference. Bright colors are acceptable but use them sparingly to avoid an overly casual vibe. Cotton T-shirts and tops with large-printed images or phrases should be avoided. Choose chinos, silk pants, or formal pants for a sophisticated, simple style. Choose one or two delicate accessories to create a polished, refined look. Earrings, delicate necklaces, bracelets, and watches are all appropriate.

TIP 02

Find your style, figure out which decade it belongs to, then go for it. Vintage clothing has nearly a century's worth of fashion. Go for it when you've found your Vintage Style, but don't go overboard. You don't want to seem like you're in a historical reenactment; you want to look retro elegant. TIP 03

Always incorporate a current element into your ensemble. If your entire clothing and accessories are vintage, skip the puddle hairdo and opt for a ponytail. Choose a pair of converse or ballerinas to soften the look of your hair, and the attire is vintage-inspired. When it comes to antique fashion, balance is crucial. Don't be afraid to accessorize your modern outfits with vintage-inspired handbags, shoes, and jewelry.
